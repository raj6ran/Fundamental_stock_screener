# Stock Screener — Investment Context & Philosophy

## Project Vision
A fundamental stock screener for NSE (India), extensible to TSX (Canada).
Built on the investment principles of **Warren Buffett**, **Anand Srinivasan**,
**Coffee Can Investing** (Saurabh Mukherjea), **Piotroski**, **Greenblatt**,
**Dorsey**, **Altman**, **Damodaran**, and **Mauboussin**.

## Investment Philosophy

### Warren Buffett — Value Investing
- Buy wonderful companies at fair prices
- Look for **economic moats** (brand, network effect, switching cost, cost advantage, patents)
- Consistent earnings over 10+ years
- High Return on Equity (ROE > 15%) sustained over time
- Low or manageable debt
- **Margin of Safety**: Buy below intrinsic value
- Management integrity and capital allocation skill
- Owner earnings > reported earnings

### Anand Srinivasan — Systematic Value
- Focus on cash-flow-generating businesses
- Avoid hype; follow numbers and unit economics
- Prefer companies with pricing power
- Look for consistent compounders with low PE relative to growth (PEG < 1)
- Sustainable competitive advantage visible in financials

### Coffee Can Investing (Saurabh Mukherjea)
- **10% revenue growth** every year for **10 consecutive years**
- **ROCE > 15%** every year for 10 consecutive years
- Buy and forget for 10+ years
- Consistent, clean, predictable businesses
- Avoid cyclicals and turnaround stories
- Focus on accounting quality (clean balance sheets)

### Piotroski F-Score — 9-point Earnings Quality
- Profitability, leverage, and operating efficiency tests
- Scores 8–9 outperform by 7.5% annually

### Altman Z-Score — Distress Prediction
- Z > 2.99 = safe, 1.81–2.99 = grey zone, < 1.81 = distress

### Joel Greenblatt — Magic Formula
- Combined rank of Earnings Yield + ROIC (cross-check, not scoring input)

### Pat Dorsey — 7 Moat Types & Moat Trend
- cost_advantage, network_effect, switching_cost, intangible_assets, regulatory, distribution, data_advantage
- Moat trend matters: widening vs eroding

### Aswath Damodaran — Reverse DCF
- Solve for market-implied growth rate. If implied >> historical, stock overpriced.
